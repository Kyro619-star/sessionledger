The problem 001

The work was real. The credit wasn't.

Music is made by people, in rooms, with their hands. But the record of who actually showed up — who wrote the topline, who fixed the bridge, who stayed until 4am dialing in the bass — that record almost never survives the session.

i. The producer

Cut from the credits.

Three people in the room. Two on the metadata. The third — the one whose drum pattern the song is built on — finds out months later, on Spotify.

ii. The topliner

No proof, no claim.

She wrote the hook. There's a voice memo somewhere on her phone. When the song blows up six months later, she has nothing to show.

iii. The engineer

Forty records, no résumé.

He's mixed forty records this year. None of them have his name attached anywhere a label or another artist can see.

— "I've been there too."
Anatomy of a record 002

Not a tag. An artifact.

A SessionLedger record is a physical-feeling document of one moment of music. It carries everything that happened — who was there, who did what, what they agreed on, when it was sealed.

It looks like a polaroid, reads like a contract, and lives forever on a public chain. Tilt the card. It moves with you.

Who it's for 006

For the people who actually make the music.

Not labels. Not lawyers. The producers, songwriters, topliners, vocalists, and engineers who show up to the room. The hands behind the work.

i. Producers · Beatmakers

The room you built.

For the producers who shape the sound — and want a verifiable trail of every session, every contribution, every co-write.

  • Beat tape provenance
  • Session-by-session credits
  • Public producer page
ii. Topliners · Songwriters

The hook you wrote.

For the writers whose contributions disappear when songs get re-cut, retitled, or re-credited — keep proof of what you wrote, the moment you wrote it.

  • Hook · melody · lyric attribution
  • Voice memo timestamping
  • Writer split locked in
iii. Engineers · Vocalists

The hours you put in.

For the mix engineers, recording engineers, session vocalists, and instrumentalists whose names rarely make it to the metadata.

  • Engineer & mix credits
  • Featured · uncredited contributions
  • Buildable studio résumé
